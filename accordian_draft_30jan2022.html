<h2>Department of Mechanical Engineering and Mechanics<br>

Strategic Plan 2022-2027 [DRAFT]

</h2>

<p> </p>

<button class="accordion">Message from the Department Head</button>

<div class="panel">

  <p>The Department of Mechanical Engineering and Mechanics (MEM) holds a place among our nation’s top private-institution mechanical engineering programs. It is my honor to lead our MEM community and to share with you our new strategic plan.<br>

<br>  

What follows is the culmination of more than one year of conversations, analysis and collaborative effort among our faculty, staff, students, alumni, and friends. I am proud of the work we have done together and deeply grateful to all the stakeholders who have engaged in this process. This plan describes MEM’s collective mission, vision, and core values and lays out four high-level goals and specific strategies and initiatives to achieve them.<br> 

<br>  

The strategic planning process heightened our understanding of the unprecedented circumstances that mechanical engineering education finds itself in today. It is one of the oldest engineering disciplines yet its tools are increasingly essential to novel applications in such areas as energy-efficient systems, advanced manufacturing, personalized medicine, ubiquitous sensing and computing, and other emerging fields. With this expansion of cross-disciplinary inquiry, a question has emerged: How do we explore the frontiers of the human-machine interface for the benefit of humankind?<br>

<br>  

This question demands that we explore how mechanical engineering education should prepare students to confront society’s greatest challenges—climate change, diseases without national boundaries, widespread human wellness and a high quality of life to go with increased longevity. It also asks us to confront important ethical issues, such as how to build equity, justice, and sustainability into our inventions, and even to consider: just because we can build something, is it wise to?<br>

<br>  

The strategic planning process was also an essential means of self-assessment. It forced us to wrestle with immediate challenges—enrollment, faculty retention, scarce and scattered space, and too-high student-faculty ratios. It necessitated our confronting frankly a complicated departmental culture. And ultimately it asked us to rethink MEM’s vision, mission, and core values in light of our responses to both of these concerns.<br>

<br>  

This plan is our response to the challenges facing our discipline and our department. It will guide our programs and curricula, hiring and professional development, governance and decision-making, resource allocation, and communications. And it will be a living document that we revisit regularly and adapt to new challenges and opportunities. Through it we will deliver on our mission and build toward our vision of a thriving MEM where we collaborate and innovate together.<br>

<br>  

Jonathan E Spanier<br>

Professor and Head<br>

</p>

</div>


<h2>Vision, Mission, and Core Values</h2> 


<button class="accordion">Vision</button>

<div class="panel">

  <p>The Department of Mechanical Engineering and Mechanics will be a vibrant and inclusive academic community of choice for diverse students and scholars—one that welcomes and supports all who are underrepresented in our field, including socioeconomically disadvantaged, into programs distinctive for integrated and collaborative education, inquiry fueled by curiosity-inspired modeling, design, and manufacturing, and the successful application of MEM discoveries and innovations to address pressing challenges facing humanity.</p>

</div>


<button class="accordion">Mission</button>

<div class="panel">

  <p>Drexel University’s Department of Mechanical Engineering and Mechanics engages students and faculty from around the world in integrated teaching & learning, research, and service that empowers them to solve complex societal problems with technical skills, creativity, and empathy.</p>

</div>


<h2>Strategic Goals</h2> 


<button class="accordion">Our strategic goals are rooted in MEM’s mission and the following principles:</button>

<div class="panel">

  <p>• Students who are guided by their interests and a sense of purpose are likely to have more rewarding professional and personal outcomes.<br> 

<br>   

• Embracing lifelong and life-wide  learning early in one’s education helps build flexibility, fearlessness, and curiosity that enhances career prospects even in the face of rapid and often disruptive changes.<br>

<br> 

• Developing resilience requires that we encourage thoughtful risk-taking, and confront the inevitable challenges or failures that arise with curiosity, patient inquiry, and renewed desire to overcome hurdles to achieving our goals.<br>

<br> 

• The best learning occurs not only with the accretion of individual knowledge but through progressively deeper cycles of engagement with a problem or case in collaboration with colleagues and peers.<br>

<br> 

• Integrating foundational coursework with ongoing exposure to real-world problems through cooperative education and other means amplifies both classroom and experiential learning.<br>

<br> 

• To ensure MEM’s long-term strength and impact, we must invest as individuals and as a departmental in building and sustaining a vibrant, collegial community of diverse backgrounds and experiences.<br>

</p>

</div>

<br>

With these principles in mind, we will pursue the following four goals:<br>

<br>

<button class="accordion">I. Create boundaryless learning environments that build empowered, resilient students</button>

<div class="panel">


<button class="accordion">1. Forge a trailblazing, adaptive, and engaging undergraduate program</button>

<div class="panel">

<p>With our guidance and ongoing support, MEM students will design personalized curricula that align their individual interests and goals with the necessary competencies, coursework, projects, and experiences to achieve them. These purpose-centric, project-based learning missions that students follow and adapt throughout their undergraduate journey will be MEM’s hallmark approach to mechanical engineering education. 

We will support this by:<br>

<br>

a. <b>Redesigning our undergraduate curriculum</b> around the key competencies of contemporary mechanical engineering and MEM students’ interests and learning missions, enriching these with other essential and emerging competencies, such as data science, AI, digital platforms, engineering ethics, advanced manufacturing and process control automation, and environmental sustainability. <br>

<br>

b. <b>Streamlining the department’s foundational course requirements</b> in order to create the latitude for students to pursue individual learning missions beginning early in their engineering studies, promote academic progress, and create cohorts around related areas of student interest.<br> 

<br>

c. <b>Increasing project-based learning opportunities</b> in areas related to common or related student learning interests, using such avenues as engineering and entrepreneurship competitions, maker spaces, problems provided by our industry partners, and projects built into MEM courses.<br>

<br>

d. <b>Fostering and growing long-term industry partnerships</b> to accelerate continuous curricular innovation and support student learning missions. <br>

<br>

e. <b>Examining how we assess student learning and success</b>, and modifying and devising new measures.</p>

<br>

</div>


<button class="accordion">2. Re-invigorate our graduate programs by creating a distinctive, attractive master’s program that helps build a strong enrollment pipeline for the PhD program</button>

<div class="panel">


<br>

a. <b>Create greater coherence for our master’s and doctoral programs</b> by identifying program-wide learning objectives and outcomes for each, and by developing a team-taught graduate core curriculum.<br>

<br>

b. <b>Expose students to the full spectrum of MEM research and build departmental community</b> by establishing a lab rotation for every entering full-time master’s student and a mentored first-year teaching assistantship and service experiences for all entering full-time PhD students.<br>

<br>

c. <b>Raise the minimum PhD stipend</b> so that it is aligned with those of our peer and aspirant institutions; <b>enhance recruitment of outstanding women and underrepresented-minority PhD students</b>; and <b>create supplemental funding targeted to raise MEM’s competitiveness</b> for the most desirable doctoral students.<br>

<br>

</div>



<button class="accordion">3. Invest in student governance and student organizations that foster department affinity and community and lower barriers to academic and career success.</button>

<div class="panel">


<br>

a. <b>Launch a student-led MEM peer-tutoring program</b> in partnership with Pi Tau Sigma and the ASME, and pilot a cognitive tutoring initiative, for example, one using Cognitive Tutoring Author Tools (CTAT).<br>

<br>

b. <b>Increase personal outreach to current and prospective MEM students—by peers, professional staff, and faculty at all levels</b>—through such activities as student recruitment, early-stage academic and career mentoring, undergraduate research, and exposure to entrepreneurship. Make such outreach a fundamental element of Department culture and a means for increasing empathy, connections, and community among all members of MEM.<br>

<br>

c. <b>Provide reliable funding for events that build community</b>; celebrate student academic, service, and leadership achievements; and showcase exceptional  accomplishments in pedagogy, mentoring, and research by students and junior scholars.<br>

<br>

d. <b>Form a student academic advisory group</b> that provides the MEM Advisory Council and Department Head with information, advice, and recommendations on student attitudes, needs, and expectations. <br>

<br>


</div>


<button class="accordion">4. Build and program the MEM Collaboratory and Learning Factory (LeaF): a signature space, “owned” by all members of the Department, that will enable boundaryless learning and inquiry, deepen student curiosity and engagement, and lower barriers to academic success. LeaF will be an inspiring, state-of-the-art physical and virtual facility that:</button>

<div class="panel">


<br>

• Activates project-based instruction;<br>

<br>

• Stimulates pedagogical experimentation; <br>

<br>

• Encourages formal and informal collaboration; and<br>

<br>

• Provides a welcoming space for MEM students, professional staff, and faculty; those of other College of Engineering departments; and anyone with interest in mechanical engineering.<br>

<br>


</div>

</div>




<button class="accordion">II. Develop a focused research agenda and enhance our research impact</button>

<div class="panel">


<button class="accordion">1. Attract, mentor, and support a diverse group of exceptional researchers</button>

<div class="panel">

<br>

a. Recruit and invest in faculty with (a) core knowledge in dynamic systems and controls, thermal and fluid systems, mechanics and materials and (b) research that addresses one or more of the following areas: energy-efficient materials, devices and systems; sustainable manufacturing; and personalized medicine and health.<br>

<br>

b. Emphasize the importance of capabilities in data science and deep physics-based and machine learning—enabling tools, applicable across numerous fields of study.<br>

<br>

c. Establish and fund early-career research positions such as postdoctoral and research fellows and comparable roles for highly-skilled technical staff.<br>

<br>

d. Create a rich set of mentoring approaches that help ensure career success for all MEM researchers, particularly those with backgrounds and experiences different from the majority of current department faculty. <br>

<br>

</div>


<button class="accordion">2. Create and maintain an inventory of the department’s research capacity and map MEM’s intellectual capabilities and infrastructure assets to current and emerging research areas.</button>

<div class="panel">


</div>


<button class="accordion">3. Secure a larger number and broader range of single- and multi-investigator research grants.</button>

<div class="panel">



</div>



<button class="accordion">4. Build relationships with entities that will amplify department research, such as co-op employers, national laboratories, institutes, industry partners, and government agencies.</button>

<div class="panel">



</div>


<button class="accordion">5. Host mechanical engineering “salons” in the form of dialogues with thought leaders on how mechanical engineering is addressing problems at the frontier of science, technology and society. Use these as forums to bring together MEM students, alumni, faculty, and staff at different stages of their education and careers; capture their imaginations; and stimulate dialogue on the implications for MEM research and teaching.</button>

<div class="panel">


</div>

</div>


<button class="accordion">III. Strengthen the Department’s outreach, networks, and talent pipeline 

</button>

<div class="panel">


<button class="accordion">1. Diversify participation in the study and practice of mechanical engineering.

</button>

<div class="panel">


<br>We will reach out to a broader pool of potential mechanical engineering students, stimulating their interest during the K-12 years by redefining what it means to be a mechanical engineer as well as what a mechanical engineer looks like, and by positioning mechanical engineering as a gateway to a vast and varied array of careers. We will make the Department an essential piece of the pipeline into the study of engineering for diverse students and out, becoming a valued source of diverse and highly capable engineering graduates for academic and professional careers. In this way, we will establish MEM as one of the nation’s premier resources for broadening understanding of and participation in mechanical engineering.<br> 

<br>

</div>


<button class="accordion">2. Form and activate advisory groups and networks that will advance MEM’s goals and mission. 

</button>

<div class="panel">


</div>


<button class="accordion">3. Ensure consistent communications and messaging with content that reenforces our core values and strategic priorities.

</button>

<div class="panel">


</div>


<button class="accordion">4. Establish formal mechanisms for mentoring faculty and professional staff, including sources of mentorship, incentives, mentor training, and evaluation of mentoring outcomes.

</button>

<div class="panel">


</div>

</div>


<button class="accordion">IV. Promote a departmental culture of shared governance built on inclusion, collaboration, and fairness

</button>

<div class="panel">


<br>1. Create a diverse, inclusive and equitable MEM community whose members take individual and collective responsibility for transparent decision-making, collaborative governance, and broad communication.<br>


<br>2. Define faculty workload holistically and analytically and share its parameters transparently and collegially.<br>


<br>3. Nurture a collaborative approach to continuous pedagogical improvement and assessment that stimulates and rewards the open, collegial, and respectful exchange of ideas and best practices, sharing of course methods and materials, and readiness to participate in peer observation and feedback.<br>


<br>4. Empower MEM professional staff and provide them the necessary professional development to work as partners with MEM faculty in activities such as:<br>

• The planning and administration of course scheduling, enrollment, credit transfers, and related student administrative matters;<br>

• Budgeting and planning; and<br> 

• The development of improved administrative policies and workflow.<br>

 

<br>5. Get to know our students better by encouraging informal and collegial interactions among faculty, students, and professional staff—for example, establishing the expectation that all MEM sophomores and first-year graduate students will host a department faculty member for a meal or coffee at the department’s expense.<br>


</div>
